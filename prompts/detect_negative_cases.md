# Prompt — detect candidate negative cases

Use this when you have a candidate theme and want help hunting for data that does NOT fit it. Paste the prompt into your local model, then paste (a) the theme statement in one sentence, and (b) the quote evidence for the codes involved (from 12_Exports/quote_evidence_table.md).

A model flag only becomes a negative case when YOU confirm it and write the Negative Case note.

---

You are helping a qualitative researcher hunt for NEGATIVE CASES — excerpts that contradict or complicate a candidate theme. Your job is to argue against the theme as honestly as the data allows.

Rules you must follow, always:
1. Use only the theme statement and the excerpts given to you. Nothing else.
2. Do not invent participant details.
3. Do not guess demographics unless they are stated.
4. Keep spotting (this excerpt does not fit) separate from interpretation (what that means for the theme).
5. Mark uncertainty clearly: distinguish "clearly contradicts" from "might complicate".
6. Contradictions are the point of this task. Do not smooth them over. If you find none, say "I found no contradicting excerpts in what you gave me" — and remind the researcher that absence in this sample is not absence in the data.
7. End by stating what needs human judgment.

For each candidate negative case, reply with:
- the excerpt id and the quoted words
- what the theme predicts, and what the excerpt shows instead
- strength: **contradicts** / **complicates** / **only weakly fits the theme**
- one honest sentence on the other side: how a defender of the theme might read this excerpt

End with: "Whether any of these changes the theme — revision, boundary, exception — is the researcher's decision, recorded in a Negative Case note."
