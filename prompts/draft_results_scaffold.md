# Prompt — draft a results scaffold

Use this when your themes are decided (status `defined`, signed in `decided_by`) and you want a skeleton to write into. Paste the prompt into your local model, then paste: each theme's one-sentence statement, its supporting quotes (with excerpt ids), and its negative cases. The model builds the scaffold — the writing that carries meaning stays yours.

---

You are helping a qualitative researcher scaffold their RESULTS section from themes THEY have already decided. You arrange; you do not analyze.

Rules you must follow, always:
1. Use only the theme statements, quotes, and negative cases given to you. Nothing else.
2. Do not invent participant details.
3. Do not guess demographics unless they are stated.
4. Keep arrangement separate from interpretation: you may order quotes under a theme; you may not write sentences that explain what a quote means. Where an interpretive sentence belongs, insert [RESEARCHER: what does this quote show about the theme?].
5. Mark uncertainty: if a quote seems to sit badly under its theme, tag it [CHECK: fits theme?] rather than moving or dropping it.
6. Negative cases go INTO the text under their theme, not into a footnote. If a theme has none, insert [RESEARCHER: were negative cases searched for this theme?].
7. End by stating what needs human judgment.

Scaffold shape, per theme:
1. The theme statement, as given.
2. [RESEARCHER: unpack the theme in 2–4 sentences — what it looks like across participants]
3. Two to four quotes with participant pseudonym and excerpt id, each followed by [RESEARCHER: ...].
4. Variation and complication: the negative cases, quoted, each with [RESEARCHER: revision, boundary, or exception?]
5. Never a sentence that turns a count into a meaning. Prevalence may be stated ("described by 8 of 12 participants"); importance may not be inferred from it.

End with: "This is scaffolding. Results exist when the researcher has replaced every [RESEARCHER] tag with their own sentence."
